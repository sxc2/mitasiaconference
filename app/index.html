The Next Chapter of Asia:
Opportunities, Innovation and Challenges

The global economy is finally recovering. The increased connectivity through new technologies have led to changing consumer behavior and transformed business practices across markets, economies and industries. The value creation in businesses has fundamentally shifted from efficient production on goods and services to greater shareholders’ return, and now with an emphasis on creativity and society. The expanding middle class in Asia has led to a tremendous rise in living standards of people whilst become a big challenge to the global environment. The disruption of natural disaster has also put more risks to society.

With these dynamics and uncertainties, what would keep the momentum of growth and drive changes in the region? Can Asia remain the engine of growth for the world’s economy? MIT Sloan Asia Business Conference 2014 will explore regional innovation, identify challenges and deliberate business opportunities facing Asia in the coming year.

Mr. Charamporn Jotikasthira

President, The Stock Exchange of Thailand

Charamporn Jotikasthira has been the president of The Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET) since June 1, 2010. In this role, he has been driving the development of SET by boosting big-cap securities, expanding its investor base, launching a new trading system, and improving SET’s efficiency to support its vision of “Empowering business and investors through our strengths to match the right financial opportunities.” He has led jointly with leaders of other ASEAN exchanges to implement the ASEAN trading link which has been in operation since the third quarter of 2012.

Charamporn has experience in both banking and capital markets. Prior to his current appointment, he had over 25 years of banking and capital market experience in the areas of technology, treasury and capital markets, credit, risk management, and investment banking. He has served in various national committees pertaining to IT, payment, and capital markets. His last position prior to joining SET was Senior Executive Vice President and Chief Information Officer of the Siam Commercial Bank PLC.

He earned his bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and MBA from the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration.

Dr. Jeanie Han

CEO, LINE Euro-Americas

Dr. Jeanie Han is in charge of running LINE in the United States, Latin America, and Europe. Under Han’s leadership, LINE has grown exponentially in these territories through successful TV and viral campaigns. For instance in Spain, the user base grew by 300 times within a span of just 6 months, elevating LINE as a true global player.

Han joined the LINE family in 2012 with extensive experience in marketing and business development. This experience has enabled her to make LINE an entertainment platform, taking digital communications beyond words on a screen and leveraging more emotional drivers to make communications more meaningful and fun. She began her career in management consulting after receiving her Ph.D. from the Marshall School of Business at USC. She frequently teaches marketing strategy at major universities in America as well as Asia.

Edward Yang

Chairman and President, iSoftStone USA

Mr. Edward Yang is the Chairman and President of iSoftStone North America Business Group. iSoftStone is a global services company providing ITO, consulting and BPO to clients in China, Japan, Korea, North America and Europe. iSoftStone is headquartered in Beijing China and currently a top 3 IT Services company in China.

Before joining iSoftStone, Edward Yang was formerly the Chairman of the Cross Shore Acquisition Corporation, a public company listed under London AIM. He was the co-founder of the company that raised US $112 Million in April 2006. In August 2007, Mr. Yang and his partner spearheaded and completed the (SPAC) acquisition and merger of a Philadelphia-based company with annual revenue over $100 Million. The acquired company is the leading provider of integrated clinical development outsourcing solutions to the bio-pharmaceutical companies. Prior to founding Cross Shore, Mr. Yang was Operating Partner at ING Baring Private Equity Partners Asia (BPEP Asia) that managed over US $500 Million of investment in the Communications and IT sectors. From 1992 to 2000, Mr. Yang served as the Corporate Senior Vice President and President of Asia Pacific Region for Electronic Data Systems (EDS Corporation) based in Plano, TX. Mr. Yang was responsible for the introduction of outsourcing and off-shoring and implementation of EDS"s core competencies to service Asia Pacific countries. Prior to joining EDS Corporation, he was Corporate Senior Vice President for Wang Laboratories responsible for the Asia Pacific and Americas region.

Mr. Yang currently serves on the Board of Regents of Pepperdine University; Board of Visitors of School of Public Policy of Pepperdine University; Advisory board of Asian Pacific American Legal Center (APALC) and Chairman of the Board of Yunnan Project Hope, building schools in the Yunnan – Tibet region for the minority children. Over the past 15 years, the project had built over 32 elementary schools in the minority region of Yunnan Province. From 2005 to 2007, Mr. Yang served on the board of Novelis Inc, an NYSE $8 Billion aluminum-rolled product company based in Atlanta, GA. Novelis Inc., which was later acquired by Birla group, India in 2007. Mr. Yang has also served on the Advisory Board of University of San Francisco McLaren School of Business and iCarnegie, a subsidiary of Carnegie Mellon University, International Advisory Board of Government of South Australia and the American Chamber of Commerce, Hong Kong.

Born in China, brought up in Myanmar and educated in Taiwan and US, Mr. Yang earned his Bachelor of Science degree from the National Taiwan University (1969), M.S. degree from University of Massachusetts (Lowell 1976) and his Presidential Key Executive MBA degree from Pepperdine University (1988). In 1997, he received the Distinguished Alumnus Award from Pepperdine University.

Head of Institute of Deep Learning, Baidu

Dr. Kai Yu is currently directing Baidu's Institute of Deep Learning (IDL), the company's Artificial Intelligence Lab. Prior to this position, he was the Director of the Multimedia Department at Baidu. Previously, he was a Department Head at NEC Laboratories America and a Senior Research Scientist at Siemens. In 2011 he was a Visiting Faculty at CS Dept of Stanford University, teaching "CS121: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence". Kai has published over 70 papers with H-index 33, was Area Chairs at top-tier machine learning conferences, e.g., ICML and NIPS, and co-authored papers receiving the Best Paper Runner-up Awards at PKDD 2005 and ICML 2013. His team has won several prestigious computer vision competitions, including PASCAL VOC 2009 and ImageNet 2010. Since 2012 his team at Baidu has worked on speech recognition, image search, deep learning, online advertising, web search, 3D street view, and robotics, has made significant contributions to a wide range of core products.

Dr. Kai received a BS and MS in Electrical Engineering at Nanjing University, China, and a PhD degree in Computer Science at the University of Munich, Germany.

Yoshikazu Kato

Fellow, Harvard University Asia Center, International Columnist

Yoshikazu Kato is currently a fellow at Harvard University Asia Center. Prior to join the Center, Kato was a Rajawali fellow at the Ash Center, Harvard Kennedy School as well as affiliated at the Charhar Institute in China. He was a visiting lecturer at School of Journalism, Fudan University in China (March-July, 2012) and visiting researcher at Keio University Shonan Fujisawa Campus in Japan (2011-2013). Kato’s research focuses recently have been Chinese democratization discourse, the rise of civil society and political development in China, Chinese nationalism and foreign policy, Japan-U.S-China relations etc.

Kato is an international columnist who writes columns in Japanese, Chinese and English. He has been a columnist in The Financial Times Chinese version (UK), The New York Times Chinese version (USA), The Nikkei Asian Review (Japan), Diamond Online (Japan) etc. During his stay in China where he got bachelor and master degree in international relations (Peking University), Kato has been a regular contributor in Chinese media which include the Southern Weekly, the Global Times, The Asian Weekly (HK), Guangzhou Daily etc as well as a commentator in CCTV (China Central Television) and Phoenix TV (HK).

Kato has published 13 books in Chinese, include Patriotic Slave, The Logic of China, The Logic of Japan,China, Do I Misunderstand You? Also in Japan, he has published 10 books: Do Chinese Really Hate Japanese So Much?, I Will Become the Bridge Over the Japan Sea, What the Chinese Are Thinking Now, A Message From North Korean Super Elites to the Japanese, Off China Theory: 56 theses on How Japanese Communicate Better with China, and so on.

Kato’s Chinese Twitter (Sina Weibo) has more than 1.5 million followers.

Dr. Phillip Kwok

Special Advisor of the China Management Education Program, MIT Sloan School of Management

Dr. Philip Kwok is a prominent businessman in Hong Kong and also in China. He joined the family controlled Wing On Group in 1970 after an outstanding career as a research physicist with IBM in the US. He was Chief Executive Officer of the Wing On Group from 1983 to 1986. During the period, Wing On became firmly established as one of the leading business conglomerates in Hong Kong with diverse interests in retail, banking and financial services, insurance and real estate development, are re-established its presence in China. Wing On is a household name in China, operating one of the most famous department stores in Shanghai since 1917 until it was nationalized in early 1950's.

Dr. Kwok has a long service record. He has served on numerous Government bodies, including the Law Reform Commission, the Hong Kong Housing Authority and the Urban Council as well as private business and charitable organizations such as the Hong Kong General Chamber of Commerce, the Hong Kong/US Economic Cooperation Committee, and the Community Chest. Dr. Kwok had also served as Chairman of the Hong Kong Philharmonic Society and President of the Hong Kong Tennis Association. He was a Vice-chairman of the Consultative Committee for the Basic Law of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China (1985-1990), and was one of the Hong Kong Affairs Advisors appointed by the Chinese Government.

Dr. Kwok is currently the President Emeritus of the Worldwide Fund for Nature of Hong Kong. Dr. Kwok was awarded the Silver Bauhinia Star (S.B.S.) by the Hong Kong SAR Government in 2003 for his outstanding civic contributions to the community of Hong Kong.

Dr. Kwok is a special advisor to the MIT/China Management Education Project, and is the MIT Sloan School of Management Program Liaison for China. And in recognition of his contribution to the Project, Dr. Kwok was appointed Adjunct Professor of the School of Management of Fudan University.

He holds a bachelor's degree in Physics from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, a doctorate from Harvard University and a certificate of the Program for Senior Executives from the MIT Sloan School of Management.
